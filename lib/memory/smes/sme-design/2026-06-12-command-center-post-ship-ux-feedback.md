# Command Center post-ship UX feedback — durable design signals

## Operator taste signals

- Keep top-level destination entry in the nav rail, not as duplicate in-surface CTA chrome.
- Favor a calm, monochrome-leaning control surface with one accent and strong signal-versus-noise separation.
- Prefer dense but breathable hierarchy over information dumps; semantic summaries come before raw artifacts, IDs, paths, or logs.
- Treat the shell as one smooth work surface with flush edges and restrained elevation, not nested framed cards.
- Use Linear-like shell behavior for the navigation rail: icon-led collapse control, resizable edge, and compact intentional chrome.

## Rejected patterns

- Labeled utility controls such as "Collapse navigation" in place of icon-led shell controls.
- Default-visible raw implementation detail in command-center rows or orientation panels.
- Multiple equal-emphasis CTAs inside a single region.
- Placeholder or redundant destination affordances in shipped navigation or home content.

## Reuse on later Command Center work

- Home must answer the four control-surface questions within 30 seconds of unassisted reading.
- Any future detail redesign should keep actions adjacent to the run, automation, or artifact they mutate.
- Breakpoint, rail-width, and motion tokens are still canon gaps; future specs should either reuse the candidates from this consult or formalize them in the design canon.
